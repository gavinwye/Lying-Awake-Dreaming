About Me

I design digital products. I spent five years learning to be a product designer. Two at Kingston College of Art and Design, and then three at The Sheffield Institute of Art and Design. I started out designing and coding, and making the odd CDROM. I then did a stint as a print designer.

In 2004 I attended Bruce "Tog" Tognazzini's Interaction Design course only to find I had been doing a lot of what he was advocating for quite some time anyway :) It's nice know your on the same page as the guy who started Apples' Human Computer Interaction Group.

I've designed and made furniture to commission and have been designing things as long as I can remember. I currently work in London Newcastle at TH_NK, a strategic digital agency as a senior experience designer. I'd prefer to just be called a designer, but hey ho.

I think Design should be taught in Art Schools I wrote about this in Design Week. A while ago. I've got a number of side projects which keep me busy when I'm not working. I also write occasionally for .Net magazine.

I'm infrequently on Twitter where I try to keep moaning to a minimum.

For examples of my work and CV take a look at my portfolio.
